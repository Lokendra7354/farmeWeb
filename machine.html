Types of Farm Machinery: Tractors:
Utility Versatile for various tasks such as plowing, tilling, and hauling.
Row Crop Tractors: Designed for precise planting, cultivating, and harvesting row crops.
Compact Tractors: Suitable for smaller farms or specialized tasks like landscaping and gardening. Tillage Equipment:
Plows: Turn and break up soil, preparing it for planting. Harrows: Refine soil, breaking up clumps and leveling the surface. Cultivators: Stir and pulverize the soil, removing weeds and aerating the soil.

Planting Equipment: Seed Drills: Plant seeds at precise depths and spacing, ensuring even crop distribution. Planters: Place seeds in rows, used for crops like corn and soybeans. Irrigation Systems: Sprinkler Systems: Distribute water evenly across fields. Drip Irrigation: Delivers water directly to the plant roots, conserving water. Center Pivots: Automated systems that move in a circular pattern to irrigate large areas.

Harvesting Machinery: Combine Harvesters: Multifunction machines that reap, thresh, and winnow grain crops. Forage Harvesters: Cut and chop forage crops like hay and silage. Balers: Compress hay, straw, or silage into compact bales for storage and transport.

Sprayers and Spreaders: Sprayers: Apply pesticides, herbicides, and fertilizers evenly across crops. Spreaders: Distribute fertilizers and lime to enhance soil fertility.

Specialty Machinery: Dairy Equipment: Milking machines, automatic feeders, and robotic milkers. Fruit and Vegetable Harvesters: Designed for specific crops, such as grape harvesters and potato diggers. Greenhouse Technology: Climate control systems, automated watering, and lighting systems.
